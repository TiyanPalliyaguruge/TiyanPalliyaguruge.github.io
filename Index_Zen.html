WHAT IS ZEN?

A state of calm attentiveness in which one's actions are guided by intuition rather than by conscious effort.

HISTORY

Zen is a school of Mahayana Buddhism that emphasizes meditation, direct insight, and simplicity.

It originated in China as Chan Buddhism during the Tang Dynasty (7th–9th century) and was influenced by Indian Mahayana Buddhism and Chinese Taoism.

The word "Zen" comes from the Sanskrit dhyāna, meaning meditation.

"To live freely, we must acquire an unfettered mind."

Shunmyō Masuno

PRINCIPLES

Zen emphasizes direct experience, mindfulness, and simplicity. Through zazen (meditation) and kōans (riddles), it fosters satori (awakening) and mushin (a clear, unattached mind).

Rooted in non-duality, it embraces wabi-sabi (imperfection) and minimalism, prioritizing personal insight over doctrine.

"Adopt a sober, steady, continuous routine."

Shunmyō Masuno

DAILY LIFE

Zen in daily life means living with mindfulness, simplicity, and presence—embracing each moment fully, letting go of attachment, and finding beauty in imperfection.

It’s about savoring food, walking with awareness, decluttering, letting go of attachments, listening deeply, and focusing on one task at a time.

"Every day is new, and new again tomorrow."

Shunmyō Masuno
